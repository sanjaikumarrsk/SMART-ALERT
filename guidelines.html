SMART ALERT – System Guidelines

1. General Usage Guidelines

SMART ALERT is an AI-based emergency detection system developed to improve public and private safety. Cameras must be installed properly with a clear field of view and reliable internet connectivity. The system should be used strictly for security and emergency response purposes.

2. How SMART ALERT Works for Government / Official Users

Government officials can access SMART ALERT through an authorized dashboard connected to public CCTV networks. The AI continuously analyzes live video feeds to detect emergencies such as fire, accidents, robbery, and explosions. When an incident is detected, the system automatically classifies it and generates real-time alerts.

3. Official User Operating Guidelines

• Secure login using official credentials
• Monitor multiple public cameras in real time
• Receive alerts with location, camera ID, and severity level
• Dispatch police, fire, or medical teams immediately
• Review stored alerts for investigation and reports

4. How SMART ALERT Works for Home / Citizen Users

Home users can connect their private CCTV cameras to SMART ALERT for continuous monitoring. The AI detects incidents such as fire, unauthorized entry, or suspicious activities. Alerts are sent instantly to the registered user, ensuring quick response even when away from home.

5. Home User Operating Guidelines

• Register and securely link personal CCTV cameras
• Place cameras at entrances and sensitive areas
• Receive instant alerts on the dashboard or mobile device
• View live camera feed during emergencies
• Contact emergency services when required

6. Privacy, Security, and Maintenance

User data and video feeds are protected using secure access controls. Regular system updates and camera maintenance are required for optimal performance. All users must comply with privacy laws and ethical surveillance practices.
